The Gap Nobody Talks About

India produces 1.5 million engineering graduates a year. Almost none of them graduate job-ready for the sector that needs them most.

The built environment is India's largest greenfield investment opportunity — pitched at Davos 2026 as the future of global capital. Green job postings are growing 22% year-on-year, but the talent supply is growing at just 12%. The gap is structural, and it's widening. This workshop is where you start closing it.

What You'll Walk Away With

Exposure. Context. Clarity.

01

The climate context your syllabus skipped

Not an activist's lecture. The actual data — IMD records, CEA emission factors, COP21 commitments — and what they mean for every building being designed in India right now.

02

Your energy footprint, calculated live

Bring your electricity bill. You'll calculate exactly how much CO₂ your home generates annually using the government's own grid emission factor. Not a full carbon audit — just the single number that makes the abstract real.

03

Where you stand on water

Benchmark your daily water consumption against WHO minimums and BIS national standards. Understand where you sit — and why this matters for every building you'll ever work on.

04

The companies hiring engineers like you

Real companies — smart water metering, robotic construction, net-zero wastewater, building automation — built on mechanical, electrical, and civil engineering. Not software. Not finance. Your domain.

05

A pathway into building systems

An introduction to the IBSP — a 12-week programme covering HVAC, green buildings, fire protection, BMS, and IoT. No hard sell. Just exposure to what a career in integrated building systems actually looks like.
